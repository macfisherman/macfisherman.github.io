Thinking About Ipv6

2018-11-29 142 words 1 min read
Ipv6 is the successor to IPv4. It essentially allows everyone to have a lot of IP addresses. I’m a Comcast subscriber. Comcast gives subscribers a /64. That means there are 2^64 (18,446,744,073,709,551,616) addresses available to assign hosts to. Because there are so many available IPs in IPv6, most modern Operating Systems assign multiple IPv6 addresses to a single computer. Currently my computer has several different types of IPv6 addresses assigned to it.
